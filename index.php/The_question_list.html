The question list

From MSSGroupWiki
Jump to: navigation, search

This page is intended for first and second year graduate members of the group (although undergraduates are encouraged to look here too).

Listed here are many questions that are relevant to our field of research. As a practicing member in the field, you should be able to answer these questions readily and in detail. In other words, these represent a base level of knowledge that would be expected at conferences, in research discussions, and at a graduate candidacy examination.

The idea is that this list of questions will provide you with some specific driving forces for your independent learning during your initial years in the lab. They are categorized for your convenience. This is not intended to be an examination, or a list of requirements, but merely a useful checklist for you to ascertain your own progress. You will find the majority of these answers to these questions in your advanced courses, by reading standard texts, and by familiarizing yourself with the literature (past and new journal articles). Don't memorize the answers here. Instead, you will want to go through the derivations yourself so that you can see things from a fundamental perspective that naturally fits into your growing understanding of the field.

What happens when you've finished answering these questions? Then it is time to start asking your own questions. You will find that some of your questions will have been addressed before, but many will have not. Indeed questions are the engine of research.

Theory[edit]

Basic statistical mechanical theory[edit]

  • What is the maximum term method? How does it apply to the evaluation of partition functions? Why is it useful as a theoretical tool?
  • Why is the grand canonical ensemble useful theoretically?
  • What are the forms (and dimensions) of all common partition functions in a classical system with continuous degrees of freedom?
  • In what ensembles can the total partition function be expressed as the product of configurational and kinetic partition functions? In other ensembles, how do the two partition functions factor?
  • How can the kinetic degrees of freedom be evaluated in the microcanonical ensemble?
  • In the canonical ensemble, how does the temperature relate to averages involving the (a) kinetic energy, and (b) potential energy? How does the pressure relate to a microscopic average?
  • What are the Einstein and Debye crystal models? What are generalized coordinates and how are they used in these models?
  • What is a harmonic vibrational free energy?
  • What is mean field theory? What is variational mean field theory, and why is it a systematic approach to developing mean field models?
  • What contributions to the partition function must be included for a diatomic gas?
  • What is a potential of mean force? Why is it called that, and why is it a free energy?
  • Why are lattice models convenient for theory?
  • What is cooperativity?
  • What is the Bragg-Williams approximation?
  • What are the techniques of quasichemical theory?
  • What is the lattice gas, and what are its properties?
  • What is the van der Waals model, and what are its properties?
  • What is Flory-Huggins theory, and what are its properties?
  • What is the virial expansion, and how does it extend from the grand canonical ensemble? How is it useful for both gases and dilute solutions of a solute?
  • How sensitive is the second virial coefficient to the specific form of the pairwise potential?
  • What are hard spheres? How is perturbation theory used to extract their properties? What named equations of state are there for hard spheres?
  • What are soft spheres? Why are they convenient to analyze theoretically?

Statistical mechanics of phase transitions[edit]

  • What does it mean that the partition function (and free energy) have nonanalytical behavior at a phase transition?
  • What is the Ising model? How can it be solved?
  • When does mean field theory work or break down for the Ising model?
  • What is the relationship between the Ising model and the liquid vapor phase transition?
  • What is universality, and what are universality classes?
  • How do response functions diverge at a critical point? What is the behavior of fluctuations there?
  • Why is the behavior at a critical point considered "nonclassical"?

Microscopic kinetics[edit]

  • What is a random walk? What are the probability distributions for it?
  • What are the basic components of classical transition state theory? How do kinetics relate to a "probability bottleneck"?
  • When does classical transition state theory break down?
  • What was Einstein's approach to Brownian diffusion?
  • How do the diffusion constant and viscosity relate to molecular averages?
  • In terms of self-diffusion, what are the "ballistic" and "diffusive" regimes?
  • What are the differences between the Green-Kubo and Einstein approaches to computing kinetic constants? How can one be derived from the other?
  • What is a (generalized) correlation function? How can a time scale be extracted from one?
  • What is the fluctuation dissipation theorem?

Statistical mechanics of liquids[edit]

  • What part of the intermolecular potential (a) gives rise to a liquid phase, (b) largely determines the structure of a liquid?
  • How does a liquid have "structure"?
  • What is the pair correlation / radial distribution function? How can it be inferred from experiment? What is a structure factor?
  • What integral equation techniques are there for approximating the pair correlation function of a liquid?
  • What is perturbation theory for liquids? What is the WCA theory?
  • What is cell theory of liquids?
  • What does the phrase "electrostatic screening" mean?
  • What is Poisson-Boltzmann theory?
  • What is a Debye length?
  • What tools are there to treat mixtures in statistical mechanics? What is lattice mixture theory? What is conformal solution theory?

Metastability[edit]

  • What is needed to define a metastable state, in terms of microstates (microscopic configurations)?
  • How is the canonical partition function modified for a metastable system? Why is the corresponding free energy necessarily lower than that of the stable state?
  • What is spinodal decomposition?
  • What is a homogeneous nucleation temperature?
  • What are the basic features of classical nucleation theory?

Simulation[edit]

Fundamentals of molecular simulation[edit]

  • What are the relative advantages of Monte Carlo versus molecular dynamics algorithms?
  • What are "equilibrium" and "production" phases?
  • What are the most time-consuming algorithms within a molecular simulation?
  • Why does a neighbor list speed a simulation?
  • What are common ways of treating the following logistic aspects of a simulation: (a) boundary condition, (b) truncation of interactions (and corrections to this), and (c) initial configuration?
  • What are two main methods for energy-minimizing a configuration, and why is one more efficient than the other?
  • How are free energies extracted from simulation? What are histograms, how are they implemented, and how do they relate to free energies?
  • What is "reweighting"?
  • What is weighted histogram analysis (also known as multiple histogram reweighting)? How is this approach used to extract free energies at (a) different temperatures, (b) different potential energy functions, and (c) along a particular reaction coordinate in the simulation (e.g., a particle-wall distance)?
  • How can histograms and histogram reweighting be used to compute phase equilibria?
  • What techniques are there to locate critical points? What is finite-size scaling?
  • Why are solid-liquid coexistence curves challenging to compute? What is the Gibbs-Duhem integration technique?
  • What is thermodynamic integration, and how is it used to extract free energy differences from simulation?
  • What is the Widom test particle method? Why challenges are there in its use?
  • How is Bennet's method optimal for calculations of free energy differences?
  • How are absolute free energies calculated? How do the ideal gas and Einstein crystal serve as "reference" states?
  • Why must electrostatic interactions be treated in a special manner?
  • How does the Ewald summation work? What adjustable parameters are there an how are they chosen?
  • What alternatives are there to the original Ewald summation in large systems?

Fundamentals of Monte Carlo methods[edit]

  • What is a Markov chain? What is detailed balance? Are other types of balance possible?
  • How are acceptance criteria developed for Monte Carlo moves?
  • What are the basic features of a Monte Carlo simulation in the following ensembles: canonical, isothermal-isobaric, grand canonical, semigrand ensemble, and microcanonical ensemble?
  • What is the Gibbs ensemble? How is it useful for phase equilibrium calculations? Where does it break down?
  • Why is the grand canonical ensemble more efficient to simulate than the isothermal-isobaric ensemble?
  • What are the challenges with insertion/deletion moves? What are some techniques to improve this?
  • Why must special Monte Carlo moves be developed to treat polymeric systems? What is configurational bias sampling?
  • What is important sampling and how is this implemented?
  • What is flat-histogram sampling (also known as multicanonical sampling)? Why is it useful?
  • What is the Wang-Landau method?
  • What is a transition matrix, how can it be constructed during a simulation, and how is it useful for extracting free energies?
  • What is hybrid Monte Carlo?
  • What is parallel tempering / replica exchange? Why is it useful?
  • What are the basic ideas of transition path sampling?

Fundamentals of molecular dynamics methods[edit]

  • What integrators are commonly used? What mathematical features make an integrator well-behaved?
  • What is the role of the timestep, and what are common values used?
  • How are rigid bonds treated? What named algorithms are useful for simulations involving them? What are the advantages of using rigid bonds as opposed to flexible ones?
  • How does one implement a constant temperature or pressure molecular dynamics simulation? Are there adjustable parameters? What do they affect?
  • What are different thermostat implementations, and which give the most accurate description of true kinetics?
  • How are diffusion coefficients and viscosities measured in simulation? What is a time origin, and how are multiple time origins used to reduce statistical error?
  • Can important sampling be applied to molecular dynamics?

Force fields[edit]

  • What is meant by an "effective" pairwise potential? Why are three-body potentials undesirable?
  • What considerations go into classical force field development? What is the role of quantum calculations? empirical fits?
  • What are the limitations of classical force fields?
  • What are polarization effects and how are they incorporated? How expensive are they computationally?
  • In what ways do the following common force fields for water differ: SPC/E, TIP3P, and TIP4P? What are the advantages/disadvantages of each?
  • What is implicit solvation? How is the solvation free energy broken into electrostatic and nonelectrostatic components?
  • What is the basic difference between Poisson-Boltzmann and Generalized Born approach to the electrostatic part of the solvation free energy, in implicit models?
  • Why are implicit models able to reproduce thermodynamic (exactly, in principle) but not kinetic properties? How can Langevin dynamics be used to improve prediction of kinetics?
  • What are some common general force fields for organic and biological molecules?

Water[edit]

Fundamentals of liquid water[edit]

  • What is unusual about water, in terms of what might be expected for a simple liquid of its molecular weight?
  • What thermodynamic and kinetic anomalies does liquid water possess?
  • What similarities does water bear to other so-called "tetrahedral liquids"?
  • How do anomalies in the heat capacity, compressibility, and expansion coefficient in water originate from anomalous fluctuations of entropy and volume?
  • What is the behavior of liquid water's response functions as it is supercooled, and what are they suggestive of?
  • What is the rationale behind a proposed liquid-liquid phase coexistence in supercooled water? Why is such a regime not experimentally accessible? What arguments exist against a liquid-liquid transition?
  • What are LDA and HDA ices?
  • What is the "TMD" line, and how does its slope relate to thermodynamic properties?
  • What ways are there to measure "structure" in water, conceptually? What is meant by "structure" in the liquid phase, where molecular configurations are transient? What relationship does local density have with water "structure"?
  • How do structural metrics of water change in (a) the bulk, with temperature and density changes, and (b) near a hydrophobic solute?
  • How do structural tendencies in liquid water compare to that of ice?
  • In what ways do the following simple theoretical models capture water's behavior: (a) the Mercedes-Benz model [e.g. JACS 120, p3166], (b) the van der Waals model supplemented with hydrogen-bonding [e.g. PRL 73, p1632], and (c) lattice models [e.g. PRE 53, p6144]?

The hydrophobic effect[edit]

  • What is the strength of a typical hydrogen bond in water in kcal/mol? in kT?
  • What makes a surface or molecule hydrophobic?
  • How does one define the hydrophobic effect? What are signatures of it in terms of temperature dependencies?
  • How does the solubulity of a small hydrophobic solute change with size, as one moves from small methane to larger molecules?
  • What is a solvent-separated state?
  • How does hydrophobic solvation relate to the probability of cavities in liquid water?
  • How does salt effect the hydrophobic interaction? What are "kosmotropes" and "chaotropes"? What is the Hofmeister series?
  • What phenomena are associated with the association of hydrophobic surfaces? What is the "true range" of hydrophobic interaction?

Supercooled liquids and glasses[edit]

Glassy behavior[edit]

  • What systems are nonergodic? What state changes can cause nonergodic behavior?
  • What is the basic phenomenology of supercooling and the glass transition?
  • What is fragility and how is it measured? How does fragility show that simple Arrhenius pictures fail for supercooled liquid dynamics?
  • What are the differences between alpha and beta relaxation in deeply supercooled liquids?
  • What is stretched exponential relaxation, and what kind of microscopic behavior is it indicative of?
  • What is dynamic heterogeneity?

Potential energy landscape theroy[edit]

  • What is potential energy landscape (PEL) theory? Why is the PEL perspective relevant to the glassy state?
  • What is an inherent structure? a basin of attraction?
  • How are inherent structures found in simulation?
  • What is the form of the canonical partition function and free energy in PEL theory?
  • What is the landscape (inherent structure) configurational entropy?
  • What is the basin enumeration function?
  • How can the configurational entropy be measured in simulation? What special techniques and approximations must be used?
  • What is a Gaussian landscape?
  • What is an entropy crisis and the Kauzmann temperature?
  • How is the Kauzmann temperature distinct from an ideal glass temperature?
  • What is the Vogel-Tamman-Fulcher relationship, and what does it imply about the landscape?
  • What is remarkable about a relationship between entropy and diffusivity?
  • How does the configurational entropy relate to dynamic properties?
  • How is the Adam-Gibbs relationship derived?
  • What are landscape metabasins? How are they relevant to dynamics?
  • What are energy landscape saddles, and how are they classified/characterized? How do they relate to dynamics?

Proteins and peptides[edit]

Fundamentals of proteins[edit]

  • How are proteins synthesized in vivo?
  • Why is protein folding a "thermodynamic", not kinetic, process?
  • What are the major kinds of secondary structure? How are they assigned?
  • What is a Ramachandran plot?
  • What are the following structural motifs (a) hairpin, (b) helix bundle, (c) beta barrel?
  • In what basic ways do amino acids differ? What special properties do select amino acids have?
  • What is a salt bridge?
  • What is a disulfide linkage?
  • What forces stabilize protein structure? Which are the most significant in encoding the native structure?
  • How is native state stability measured?
  • What evidence is there that a protein folding is cooperative and similar to a first-order transition?
  • What is a chaperone and how does it help folding?

Folding kinetics[edit]

  • What are "two state" folders? "three state"?
  • What are the folding times of ultrafast folders? typical folders? slow folders?
  • What is a folding mechanism? What are some proposed mechanisms?
  • Why are protein transition states considered "ensembles"? How do the kinetics of protein folding differ from simple chemical reactions?
  • What is a phi value, how is it measured experimentally, and what does it mean?
  • What is folding heterogeneity?
  • What is the zipping and assembly folding mechanism?

Protein theory[edit]

  • What is a Go model? a HP model?
  • What is a random energy model (heteropolymer collapse model)?
  • What is a Z-score?
  • What is funnel theory?
  • What are master equation models?
  • What is an RMSD measurement useful for?

Protein homology and bioinformatics[edit]

  • How is homology measured?
  • What typical levels of sequence homology are considered highly homologous, and likely to have the same fold?
  • What is bioinformatics? structural genomics? the PDB?
  • What is a sequence alignment, and how does it work? What is a multiple sequence alignment?
  • What are the most successful bioinformatics-based protein structure prediction methods to date? In general terms, how do these work?
  • What are statistical folding potentials and how are they developed?

Protein simulations[edit]

  • Why is protein folding challenging to simulate?
  • What small proteins have been folded using supercomputing resources, and what have we learned from these studies?
  • How does replica exchange molecular dynamics work, and why is useful for protein simulations?
  • What are the advantages and disadvantages of using implicit solvation for protein folding?
  • What are commonly used force fields for proteins?
  • What is a clustering algorithm (applied to a protein trajectory) and how does it work?
